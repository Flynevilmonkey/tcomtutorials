Reformating a Hard Drive for Mac

  1. Backup all data on your external hard drive. ALL DATA WILL BE ERASED.
  2. Open the DIsk Utility app in the Utilities folder inside the Applications folder.
  3. Make sure your external drive is plugged in and powered on. It will show up in the left panel of the Disk Utility app.
  4. Click on your dive. Then, click on the Partition tab in the upper middle of the app.
  5. Under “Partition Layout:” is a drop-down menu. Click on that and choose however many partitions you want.
    • Different partitions can be formatted for different operating systems.
    • If you would like a Windows partition, choose more than one in the Partition Layout. drop-down and format one of the partitions as “ExFat.”
  6. To the right, give your partition(s) a name.
  7. Under that is another drop-down menu where you can choose the format. Pick “Mac OS Extended (Journaled).”
  8. Choose a size for your partition(s) just below that. You can type in a size in the text box or grab the box to the left with your mouse and drag to the desired size.
  9. If everything is set, click on Apply.
  10. A dialog box will come up, explaining what you are about to do, and ask if you are sure. If you are, go ahead and click on Partition.
  11. If you are doing this on a lab computer you may need admininstrative privileges to complete step 10. If that is the case, please see the lab technician in BC 194G.

Some things to be mindful of:

Right click (Control+click) on your drive on the desktop. Choose Get Info. It will tell you what format your drive currently is.

NTFS is read-only on Mac computers. You will NOT be able to write any data to your hard drive if it is formated as NTFS.

If your drive is MS-DOS (FAT), you may be able to read and write to it, but there are limitations. 4 GB is the maximum size single file you can transfer.

Exfat is the most compatible Windows format on a Mac computer. It can read and written to using a Mac.
